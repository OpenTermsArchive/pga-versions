Facebook Live Policies

1\. Overview

**Understanding our policies**

The following terms, as well as our [Data Policy](https://www.facebook.com/about/privacy/), [Community Standards](https://www.facebook.com/communitystandards/), and [Terms of Service](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms), apply to all users on Facebook. You are responsible for ensuring that your content complies with all applicable laws, statutes, and regulations.

2\. General Policies for Facebook Live

**Prohibited Practices**

Facebook Live must not be used to mislead users that specific footage is occurring in real time when it is not.

Example:

*   Misrepresenting footage of an event that occurred a year ago as currently happening

**Inaccurate Location**

Facebook Live must not be used to mislead users that specific footage is happening in a particular locale when it is not.

Example:

*   Misrepresenting footage from a different country as footage from the United States

**Pre-recorded Content**

All pre-recorded content contained in a broadcast streamed to Facebook Live must be clearly marked as pre-recorded. For broadcasts that contain a mixture of live footage and pre-recorded footage, only the pre-recorded footage must be marked as pre-recorded.

Example:

*   Adding a graphic to mark specific footage as pre-recorded

*   Including a disclaimer in the video description or title that specific content is pre-recorded

**Looping or Static Content**

Facebook Live should not be used to broadcast looping videos, static images, or poll-only videos.

Example:

*   Streaming a 20-minute clip that is continuously looped

*   A broadcast that is limited to a photo and has no other auditory or visual elements

*   A broadcast that only contains a poll

Facebook Live policies and guidelines
=====================================

In order for creators to have the best possible experience on Facebook Live, we have established a set of rules and guidelines that outline our policies for Live broadcasts.

All content on Facebook must comply with our [Community Standards](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/https%3A%2F%2Fl.facebook.com%2Fl.php%3Fu=https%3A%2F%2Ftransparency.meta.com%2Fpolicies%2Fcommunity-standards%2F) and [Terms](https://www.facebook.com/legal/terms). You are responsible for ensuring that your content complies with all applicable laws, statutes and regulations.

However, there are additional rules and guidelines specific to Facebook Live.

Facebook Live policies and guidelines
-------------------------------------

Your Live content must follow these guidelines in order to be eligible for distribution or monetization.

**Note:** [You can report or give feedback on broadcasts](https://www.facebook.com/help/462017383994422/).

**Facebook Live should not be used to:**

*   Broadcast looping videos, static images or poll-only videos.
    
*   Mislead users that specific footage is occurring in real time when it is not.
    
*   Mislead users that specific footage is happening in a particular locale when it is not.
    

**Live broadcasts with pre-recorded content must:**

*   Be clearly marked as pre-recorded. For broadcasts that contain a mixture of live footage and pre-recorded footage, only the pre-recorded footage must be marked as pre-recorded.
    

**Facebook Live broadcasts with music must:**

*   Minimize the number of distinct music pieces and full-length recorded tracks and keep overall music duration to a minimum (under 30 minutes).
    
*   Have a visual component; recorded audio should not be the primary purpose of the video.
    
*   Have approval from the rights holder(s) to use their music on Facebook Live.
    

Live performances (ex: singing or playing music on an instrument) are generally not restricted so long as they do not contain pre-recorded backing materials that match protected content.

**Content presented in the following formats cannot be monetized:**

*   Static videos.
    
*   Content that contains one static image and little to no motion.
    
*   Content posted for the sole purpose of increasing engagement by asking people to react to questions posed by the content.
    

Depending on the features available to you, other Facebook terms and policies may also apply to your use of Facebook Live, including [Meta Advertising Standards](https://www.facebook.com/policies/ads) and [Branded Content Policies](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/221149188908254). Please refer to our [Content Monetization Policies](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/1348682518563619?id=2520940424820218) or [Partner Monetization Policies](https://www.facebook.com/business/help/169845596919485) for general creator monetization policies and best practices. Video content must comply with Meta’s [Privacy Policy](https://www.facebook.com/policy).
